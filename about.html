Hi, I’m Ennis Ogidan.

EARLY LIFE

Born and raised in Clapham, South West London, to Nigerian parents, I attended St Mary’s RC Primary school from 2003 to 2011. It was at St Mary’s where I discovered my love for the beautiful game, football. By the time I got to 11 years there had been a lot of changes in my life. I moved from Clapham to Norbury, I was transitioning into the next stage of my education (secondary school) at St Joseph’s College, and I started making money for myself on a consistent basis (more on that later).

St Joseph’s made me. I discovered the world of anime and manga, picked up a completely new sport in the form of basketball and realised my passion for technology and the digital world all during my 6 years at the school. St Joseph's is also where I learnt to grind, hustle and make something out of nothing through my cookie resell business. As soon as I got to secondary school I realised that I couldn't rely on people for money and that I'd need to learn how to make my own. This led me to have a genius plan. Every morning I would buy multiple packs of Sainsbury's bakery cookies at a price of £1 for a 5 pack. At school, I would then resell each cookie for 50p resulting in a profit of £1.50 per pack. This business I had taught me ‘the value of money’ and made me realise what my parents meant when they said, “money doesn’t grow on trees”.
